1) Addressable LED

SK6812-GRBW, SMD-5050 as an example

VSS — Ground
DIN — Data Input (-0.5 $\sim$ VDD + 0.5)
VDD — Power
DOUT — Data Output

The speed of data transmission: 800 kHz

DIN - DOUT delay 500 ns

GRBW frame
8 bit - G + 8 bit - R + 8 bit - B + 8 bit - W
32 bits to control the colour and brightness

Several paras should be noted:

  • Brightness (mcd): 1800 $\sim$ 2500
  • Delivering current: 16.5 mA
  • Interval: 7 - 10 mm
  • Forward voltage
  • Forward current
  • IREF: contant-current values
    When it comes to an array of leds, resettable fuse, TVS, large capacitor should be added.

2) Pure white LED

Constant drivers are used to control on/off.

TLC5947, 24 channels LED drivers

TLC5973D, 3 channels
IS31FL3218, 9 channels

3) mcd, lm, lux

  • Coloured LEDs are usually spec’d in mcd, which are used for directional indicators.

  • White LEDs are spec’d in lumens for area illumination
    lumens $\approx I \Omega$ with $I$ in c, $\Omega \approx 2\pi (1 - \cos(\theta/2))$.

    For 180 mcd at view angle $\theta = 60 ^\circ$
    lumens $\approx $ 0.15 lm

    • To estimate `brightness’ at a distance $r$:
      Illuminance (lux) $\approx I/r^2$.

    lux is used to evaluate the brightness on-axis at the centre of the beam.
